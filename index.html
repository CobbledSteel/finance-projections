How this works & assumptions
  • Fair comparison: both scenarios deploy the same total cash each month. The down payment + closing costs that the buyer spends are instead kept invested by the renter. Whichever scenario has the lower monthly housing cost invests the difference.
  • Income & investing: a fixed % of (growing) income is invested every month in both scenarios, so the chart reflects total net worth including ordinary savings, not just the housing decision.
  • Buying costs: mortgage principal & interest, property tax, home insurance, HOA, and maintenance. Principal payments build equity (tracked separately). Home value grows at the appreciation rate.
  • Equity: home value − remaining loan balance. With "subtract selling costs" on, net worth reflects what you'd actually walk away with if you sold (realtor fees, etc.).
  • Renting costs: rent (grows yearly) + renter's insurance.
  • Wait before buying: during the wait the buyer rents the same place and keeps saving/investing, while the home keeps appreciating, so waiting longer means a higher purchase price and a larger down payment. The Auto-set button finds the earliest month your savings cover that (rising) down payment + closing costs.
  • Returns: investment and appreciation rates compound monthly; rent and income step up once per year.
  • Not modeled: income taxes, mortgage-interest / property-tax deductions, capital-gains tax, PMI for low down payments, and rent control. Returns are nominal & pre-tax unless you adjust for inflation. Treat this as a planning estimate, not financial advice.
